Onam Celebration with the South Indian Cultural Club: A Day of Food and Fun
10-15-2024
As a board member of the South Indian Cultural Club at Cal Poly, I recently had the incredible opportunity to help organize and host our annual Onam celebration. For those who might not be familiar, Onam is a traditional festival celebrated in Kerala, my home state in India. It’s a time of joy, togetherness, and, of course, amazing food! The event was one of the most memorable experiences I’ve had with the club so far. From the moment we started planning, we knew we wanted to make it special for everyone who attended, whether they were familiar with Onam or experiencing it for the first time. The best part? I got to contribute by helping prepare some of the delicious traditional dishes that are central to the festival. Onam is known for the Onam Sadya, a grand feast that includes a variety of vegetarian dishes served on a banana leaf. From avial (a mixed vegetable curry with coconut) to sambar, and sweet treats like payasam, the food truly represented the rich culinary heritage of Kerala. It was such a rewarding experience to see everyone enjoying the food, some for the first time and others getting a nostalgic taste of home. Aside from the food, what really made the event stand out was the vibrant atmosphere. People from different backgrounds came together, bonded over a shared love for culture and food, and just had fun. It was heartwarming to see new friendships form, with people laughing, sharing stories, and enjoying the cultural activities we had planned. The traditional games were a huge hit, and it was great to see everyone so engaged and spirited. For me, the most exciting part of the event was watching everyone’s reactions. Whether it was their first bite of a Sadya meal or their participation in one of the cultural games, the joy and excitement in the room were palpable. Being a part of the event, cooking for it, and watching people enjoy both the food and the festivities made the day even more special for me. I also got the chance to meet and talk to so many new people, building connections that will last beyond this one event. Organizing the Onam event reminded me why I love being a part of the South Indian Cultural Club. It's not just about sharing our culture with others, but also about creating moments of togetherness and joy that bring everyone closer. I’m already looking forward to the next event, but this Onam celebration will always hold a special place in my heart.
